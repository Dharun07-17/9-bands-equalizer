63 Hz & 125 Hz: Controls the heavy, deep sounds like bass drums, bass guitar, and sub-bass. Boosting adds warmth and fullness, while cutting removes mud or boominess. 

250 Hz, 500 Hz, & 1k (1000 Hz): Houses the core of most instruments and human vocals. Adjusting these affects how "hollow" or "thick" a track sounds.

2k Hz: This represents the upper-midrange. Boosting it slightly helps vocals and instruments cut through a busy mix for better clarity. 

4k Hz & 8k Hz: Controls brilliance, detail, and speech intelligibility. Boosting brings out the crispness in guitars and cymbals.

16k Hz: Handles high-end air, sparkle, and ambient room sound.
